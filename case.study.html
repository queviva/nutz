Parents Killed, Children Orphaned, Investigators Ask: "What Was Their Genotype?"
A lot goes wrong in the wild, wild, world of neuroscience. This true story of murder, mayhem, and intro-level statistics, will convince even the most ardent skeptic that math will never help in later life, but it may help resolve technical fiascos.
People breed mice. Medical researchers need specimens of specific genetic constructions in order to experiment on the types of genetics which most accurately represent the diseases they study. So, medical researchers breed mice a lot, and need to be certain of their genetic makeup.
As you might have already guessed, the mice typically die during the course of the experiment. Here, "typically" means "basically every single time without exception." When researchers kill mice as part of an experiment, they call that "sacc-ing" (short for "sacrificing"). Most medical research involves a frantic stream of raising, breeding, and killing mice - and not always in that order!
Try to keep in mind how much borderline chaos a fast paced lab inspires. Someone who earned a graduate degree in biology does not automatically come with better organizational, or project-management, skills just because that would improve the quality of labwork.
